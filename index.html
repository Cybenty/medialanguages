Text Information

Text information in media refers to the written content that conveys messages, ideas, and narratives to the audience. This can include headlines, captions, articles, scripts, and any other form of written communication used in various media formats such as newspapers, websites, television, and social media. Understanding text information is crucial for analyzing how media shapes public perception and influences societal norms.


Denotation

Denotation refers to the literal, explicit meaning of a word or text.

Connotation

Connotation refers to subjective interpretation of text based on context and emotion.

Classifications

Non-Fiction

Based on facts, reality, and verifiable information.

Fiction

Writers' imagination and creative storytelling.

Production Process

  1. Writer selects a topic or an issue worth writing about.
  2. Writer does a thorough gathering of information about the topic.
  3. Writer constructs an outline of the topic and composes the draft.
  4. Writer goes back to the draft and develops it into a full text.
  5. The writer turns in the article to the editor or publisher.

Assessing and evaluating text information

  1. Accuracy and factuality - Text should be precise and must be based on facts.
  2. Objectivity - Text information should not contain any biases, prejudice, or discrimination.
  3. Language appropriateness - The level and kind of languages should be appropriate for the target.
  4. Curriculum alignment - The textbook material should be consistent with the curriculum and international standards.
  5. Style and format- Most books are based on the Chicago manual of style when it comes to all editorial concerns like capitalization, punctuation, and citation on the other hand, academic papers follow the American psychological association style, newspaper follow a particular house style.

Advantages

  1. Covers wide variety of topics
  2. One can access text information easily
  3. It is well-researched and comprehensively discussed

Limitation

  1. The book should be updated regularly to ensure accuracy and timeliness
  2. Text information can be lengthy making it difficult or tiresome to read


Media Codes

Commonly have an established meaning, denotation or connotation to the target audience


Types of media codes

  1. Symbolic codes

    1. Audiences based
    2. The meaning of the product is not based on the product itself but on the interpretation of the audience
    3. The symbolic codes in media include setting, mise en scene, acting and color
      1. Setting- time and place
      2. Mise en scene - french term everything within the frame
      3. Acting - portrayal of the actors
      4. Colors- considerations are highly connotative when it comes to interpretations it is also usually associated with cultural aspects

  2. Technical codes

    1. Refers to codes specific to a media form alone
    2. Camera work operation positioning and movement for desired specific effects editing the process of selecting operating and ordering images and sound audio expression

  3. Written codes

    1. Are the formal written language used in creating a media product
    2. Includes the printed language the text visible with the media frame which is the text you can see.




Media Conventions

Conventions refer to the possible methods in which codes are organized in a product.


Types of conventions

Form convention

Are ways in which the types of media codes are expected to be arrange. For instance, the title and main casts are expected to appear at the beginning of a movie while the credits are expected to appear at the end

Story conventions

Refer to the basic structures of narratives. Examples of story conventions narrative structures, character constructions, and point of view


Genre conventions

Refer to the common use of the elements of narratives such as the characters, setting s or themes in a certain type of media Genre conventions can be formal or thematic and are usually linked to the expectations of the audience




Media producers stakeholders and audience

Aside from media language, media practitioners also look into other elements such as the producers, the stakeholders, and the audience

Media Producers

refers to the people who initiate, plan , and produce media
They need to have the skill in assessing the media texts and a thorough understanding of the target product; and the processes that go into creating the products

Stakeholders

refer to the people or organizations that share the same interests or intentions with media producers

The Audience

Significant element in delivering media text, cosumes the media

Audience engagement

Reaction of the audience to the media text. Different people react in varied ways to the same text

Audience Expectations

Anticipation of the audience about the text
Authors may satisfy or shatter the audience's expectations

Audience foreknowledge

exact information which audiences brings about the media output

Audience identification

connection built by the media text to the audience

Audience placement

Strategies producers use to make the audience feel that the media text is made specifically for them

Audience research

Monitoring of the audience before, during, and after the production of the media text
